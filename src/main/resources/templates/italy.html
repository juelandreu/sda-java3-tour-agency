It’s hard to find a destination with a richer history than Italy. Taking its place at the very heart of antiquity, today there’s a rich array of ancient Italian tourist attractions often dating back thousands of years. But with literally hundreds of places to choose from, selecting the top ten tourist attractions in Italy is no simple task. So if you’re seeking inspiration for your trip – be it a city break in Rome then our selection of the top 3 sights in Italy will get you started. We’ve included some of the most famous Italian tourist attractions as well as a few fantastic sights you may not know. And if that doesn’t sate your appetite for Italian attractions, our expanded list of historical sites in Italy will give you loads more to discover.

Itinerary

Day 1

Roman Treasure Trove

Rome is a city rich in antiquity and history, brimming with decadent restaurants and vibrant culture. When you arrive at the airport, your private transfer will be waiting to bring you into the charismatic city and the cradle of the Roman Empire. Narrow alleyways twist around antique buildings opening up to hidden piazzas, and your hotel is located along the cobbled streets and close to the bubbling sounds of the iconic Trevi Fountain.

Day 2

Jewels of Rome

Rome is a city full of wonder, from ancient architecture to priceless artwork decorating historic palaces. Your guide will lead you on a morning walking tour from the massive concrete dome of the Pantheon to the elegant marbled piazza around the Spanish Steps. In the afternoon, you make your way to the Vatican Museum for a self-guided exploration of the majestic gilded hallways.

Day 3

Sensational Summers

In the morning, your guide will meet you at your hotel and escort you 18 miles east of the city to the exceptional splendor of Tivoli. Step into a perfect example of ornate Italian gardens in Villa d’Este, which countless European gardens have emulated over the years. The terraced grounds were converted into a luxurious retreat in the 16th century. You can take your time along the 426-foot Avenue of the Hundred Fountains, basking in the magnificent artistry in every luxuriant detail.

Day 4

Highlighting the Renaissance

In the morning, you make your way to Roma Termini Station. After your board your first-class car, enjoy your view as the golden hills around the city turn into the lush, rolling greenery of Tuscany. Your train will stop at Santa Maria Novella Station in Florence, and your hotel will be nestled in the historic center of the sensational city. Enjoy your first glance at the city as chic cafes offer tables outside where you can watch the world go by. See the Florence Cathedral's dome, known as the Duomo, peeking its head over the buildings from almost anywhere in the city.

Day 5

Hidden Paths

Enjoy a slow morning and a leisurely breakfast, and then meet your guide for your Tuscan food tour. Spend a perfect morning strolling through the streets of Florence, discussing classic Tuscan dishes, olive oil, and wine. Stop at several of the best markets Florence has to offer, sampling Tuscany's most prized possession of flavors. Cross paths with your second guide who will bring you to the Duomo and the Uffizi Gallery. From the outside of the Gallery, notice the small archway connecting Palazzo Vecchio to the Uffizi. From the outside, the archway is unassuming, but it is the beginning of the Vasari Corridor. The corridor provided safe and easy access for the Medici's to move between the governor's palace and their residence.

Day 6

Tuscan Flavors

After breakfast, you will make your way into the famous Tuscan hills, where the rolling countryside blossoms with cypress trees, verdant grass, and endless rows of grapevines. Your exclusive tour of a private Renaissance villa takes you into the heart of Chianti, and the slower pace and tranquil atmosphere is immediately apparent. Your host will lead you to a large wooden table on a terrace overlooking the garden with a traditional Tuscan spread of simple meats and cheeses. Enjoy a sample of the Badia a Passignano Chianti Classico, and the pleasures of the wine embody the majesty of the day.

Day 7

Pearl of the Lagoon

In the morning, sip your espresso and think about the endless marvels of the city as you prepare to depart. Make your way to the Santa Maria Novella Station and board your first-class compartment on the way to the magnificent city of Venice. When you arrive, a water taxi will take you to your lavish accommodations in the heart of the city. The labyrinthine cobbled streets hide historic gems as carnival masks decorate shop windows adding to the luster of the city’s antique wealth and legendary nobility. Upon arrival, settle in and enjoy your first views of the city.
